Privacy Policy — Imposter

Last updated: 25 May 2026

Imposter ("the app") is a party word game published by the Imposter development team. This page explains what data the app handles, why, and your choices. We try to collect as little as possible.

What we collect

  • Anonymous account ID. When you open the app we sign you in to Firebase Authentication as an anonymous user. This gives your device a random, opaque ID so the server can attach you to a game room. We do not ask for your email, phone number, name, or any other identifier.
  • The display name you type in. You choose a name ("Alex", "Sam", etc.) so other players in your room can see who is who. This is stored only while the room exists and is visible only to other players in the same room.
  • Game-state data. While a room is active we store the room code, theme, current phase, clue text you type, votes you cast, and whether you have peeked your role card. This lives in Google Firestore and is shared in real time with the other players in the same room. It is not used for advertising or analytics.
  • No tracking, no analytics, no ads. The app contains no third-party advertising SDKs, no analytics SDKs (Google Analytics, Firebase Analytics, etc.), and does not sell or share data with anyone.

Who can see what

  • Players in your room see your chosen display name and your clue text. They cannot see your secret role until the game reveals it.
  • The game host's device sees the role assignments because role assignment runs on the host (security rules prevent other players from reading the same data).
  • Imposter developers and Google (as our hosting provider) have operational access to the Firestore database for the purpose of running the service. We do not inspect individual rooms.

How long we keep it

Game rooms are best-effort and short-lived. When a player leaves we delete their member record from the room. When the game ends the room remains for a short window so players can see the result, then becomes inactive. We do not maintain long-term player profiles.

Children

The app is intended for a general audience. It does not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. Because the sign-in is anonymous and we collect no PII, there is nothing for parents to request deletion of beyond the room data, which expires on its own.

Your choices

  • You can leave any room at any time by tapping the leave icon — we delete your member record immediately.
  • You can clear the app's data from your device's settings to regenerate your anonymous ID.
  • To request deletion of any room data still in Firestore, contact us at the email below.

Third-party services

  • Google Firebase (Authentication, Firestore, Hosting) — see Firebase Privacy.

Changes to this policy
